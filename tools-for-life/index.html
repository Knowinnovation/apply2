Let's Talk about Health Data Sharing

A Deliberation Day event run by the University of Sheffield

Key Dates

  • Application Deadline 19th May 2021
  • Applicants Receive Invitation to Join the Event 21st May 2021
  • Asynchronous work starts Monday 24th May 2021
  • Final interactive session Thursday 27th May 2021, 10:00–13:00 BST

More Information

The event is being organised by the Tools for Life: Data Sharing and Public Health project, at the University of Sheffield, and funded by the Wellcome Trust Public Engagement Fund. More information about the project is available here.

Overview

The purpose of the event is to engage the public in a conversation about the benefits and risks of sharing patient data for public health. The event will involve talks, animations, and infographics based on interviews and a survey conducted by the Tools for Life: Data Sharing for Public Health project team.

Participation

Participation in the event constitutes agreeing to undertake a series of short tasks during the week beginning Monday 24th May 2021, and ending with attending a short workshop on Thursday 27th May 2021. The total time commitment is expected to be roughly 4½ hours during that week, including 3 hours on Thursday 27th, between 10am and 1pm. Participants will be expected to reflect, discuss, and vote electronically on the content of media shared with them, and to participate in closing session to share their thoughts.

To fully participate in the event, you will need to use a laptop or tablet with internet access, and be able to join a Zoom meeting. You can check that this will work for you before registering by joining a test Zoom call.

Participants will be reimbursed £40 to compensate for their time following the conclusion of the event.

Registration

You can register by sending an email to toolsforlife@sheffield.ac.uk . You will receive an information sheet and consent form, which must be signed and returned by email. After signing the form, participants will be directed to a digital platform for the event itself. NHS Ethics has been sought and approved for the event.
