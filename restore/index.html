Restoring Atmospheric Methane Levels

We seek reduction of atmospheric methane levels to change the rate of climate change in our lifetimes.

Reducing atmospheric methane levels provides an immediate reduction in climate warming rates. There are several viable strategies for mitigating some human-derived methane sources today, but challenges remain to achieve this goal. Accelerating methane removal from the atmosphere presents additional technical and socioeconomic hurdles.

We invite you to help articulate urgent needs in technology, social science, and policy that will facilitate a rapid reduction of atmospheric methane levels. The output of the workshop is intended to inform global research strategies and methane action networks.

We recognize that implementation paths to a lower-methane atmosphere are complex, interdisciplinary, and have far-reaching implications. As such, we are bringing together engineers, scientists, policy makers, commercial partners, social scientists, and economists to articulate solutions and implementation paths, delineate funding needs to make these a reality, and initiate projects that will make a difference.

Join us to make a tangible difference in global warming rates that can be measured within our lifetime.

Goals

  • Frame the methane abatement opportunity and dominant source characteristics to motivate researchers across the globe.
  • Identify research synergies and opportunities to promote rapid development and implementation of methane abatement or mitigation solutions that could be deployed within the next 5 years.
  • Identify major hurdles to implementation and propose strategies to overcome these (e.g., policy, investment, social acceptance).
  • Produce a brief report (1-page executive summary + 5 page report) summarizing research needs and opportunities.
  • Produce a table of research funding needs and priorities.

    • Technology Needs Workshop 1 (April 7 & 8)
