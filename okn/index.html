Overview

OKN, the open knowledge network, is a concept that envisions connecting many public, government, and other open datasets into one large connected information infrastructure. The benefit of creating this infrastructure is that government agencies, private organizations, academics, and anyone can better access and utilize open data to drive evidence-based policies, develop novel AI capabilities, and tackle societal problems.

The focus of the OKN innovation sprint is to construct a roadmap to a prototype OKN through the development of compelling use cases to illustrate what might be possible with a fully functional open knowledge network.

In the first phase, participating scientists, government agencies, and industry representatives will form teams and design use cases.

In the second phase, groups will dive deeper into those use cases to develop road maps to support the realization of the use case through an open knowledge network.

In the third phase, groups will present their work and develop concepts for integrating the use cases as foundational components of a prototype open knowledge network.

The Challenge

Large knowledge structures with natural language query interfaces will enable new modes of data-driven discovery with the potential to impact science, education, business, and society in a manner comparable to the world wide web. Knowledge graphs are already widely used in industry for consumer services and enterprise applications, but are proprietary systems. To enable the maximum benefit to society, this sprint will lay the foundation for a nonproprietary shared knowledge infrastructure. To ensure impact, use cases will be built based on government agency data priorities and building on existing data infrastructures, but identifying challenges that can best be solved with integration of multiple data sets managed by multiple organizations.

For more insights see Open Knowledge Network: Summary of the Big Data Interagency Working Group Workshop, 2017 [https://www.nitrd.gov/pubs/open-knowledge-network-workshop-report-2018.pdf] and the Open Knowledge Network projects funded by the NSF Convergence Accelerator Track A [https://www.nsf.gov/news/special_reports/announcements/090320.jsp]

Stakeholders

This project will require input from a broad collection of scientific disciplines, as well as input from government agencies that publish and use open data (which is nearly all of them).

In addition to academic and government participants, industry partners are key stakeholders, given their expertise and experience, as well as their position as users of a possible future open knowledge network.

The Opportunity

This event series is unlike your typical top-down speaker/panel dominated conferences.

  • Participants will learn from collaborations across sectors and disciplines.
  • The project is interested in real problems and novel solutions.
  • This is a chance to shape the development of how a major technological resource develops.

Meeting Outcomes

The work done will produce an integrated report, outlining key opportunities and challenges and possible next steps to establish the prototype of an open knowledge network.
