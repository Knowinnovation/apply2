Overview

The National Institute of Impossible Aerodynamics will host the "Porcine Aviation Ideas Lab," an initiative to explore breakthrough directions for airborne livestock. We invite prospective innovators to apply to attend this five-day intensive workshop. As part of the application, individuals will be asked to submit a brief executive summary outlining a compelling research direction toward achieving sustainable and safe flight for the domestic pig.

The Challenge

For centuries, the phrase "when pigs fly" has been the ultimate metric of the impossible. We have seen impressive advances in the fields of genetic engineering, lightweight materials, and propulsion systems, yet a pig that functions with suitable levels of aerodynamic efficiency does not yet exist. To achieve this, a biological or mechanical system must reliably lift a standard farm pig, allow for controlled navigation, and meet pressing agricultural needs ethically. Creating such systems will require significant advances in three key areas:

  • Foundational theory of porcine aerodynamics, to understand the art of the possible and bound the limits of livestock levitation.
  • Bio-engineering and lightweight augmentation, to predictably build systems or biological adaptations that work safely in real-world farm environments.
  • Human-swine aviation teaming, to enable systems to serve as intuitive, trustworthy transport or agricultural partners.

Event Outcomes

The ultimate output of the workshop will be the identification of promising areas for future research in porcine aviation. For each area, participants will explore open issues, potential research questions, desired impact, and needed expertise to make progress. Participants will make a presentation and provide a companion white paper. We will consider these outputs to form our future funding strategy, which will likely include new seed grants for the most promising "Flying Pig" exploration projects.

    About the Event

    The Porcine Aviation Ideas Lab will consist of a highly interactive and collaborative workshop. During this event, participants will work together to envision new research directions that could lead to practical flying pigs. Participation is limited to approximately 50 people. The workshop will include discussions and guided brainstorming sessions with industry, academic, and agricultural experts.

    • Engage constructively in dialogue with facilitators to develop collaborative research themes.
    • Form small interdisciplinary groups to pitch and refine concepts for aerodynamic swine.
    • Focus on solution-driven discussions after targeted plenary lectures by leading aviation and zoology experts.

    Who Should Apply

    We are bringing together experts in Aerospace Engineering, Evolutionary Biology, Swine Veterinary Medicine, Lightweight Materials, Propulsion, and Meta-Cognition. It is anticipated that the interdisciplinary collaborations formed during this event will result in the identification of new and promising areas for future research. Original thinkers from outside traditional academia and industry are also highly encouraged to apply.

    How to Apply

    To apply, please complete the online application form by March 15, 2024. You will be asked to provide your CV, a brief statement of interest, and a 500-word summary of your wildest, most scientifically sound idea for getting a pig off the ground.
