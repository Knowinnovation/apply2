Overview

To maintain global leadership in biological science (BioSci) innovation, new processes are needed that improve collaborations among academic, private, and governmental BioSci stakeholder communities to facilitate the translation of basic research to innovation and to better inform evidence-based practices. The current and next generation of researchers lack clear support pathways and training opportunities for careers in use-inspired research and science-informed practices. This NSF-funded workshop will build new connections among key BioSci stakeholder communities. The workshop will consist of virtual (for an open number of participants) and in-person (for 120 participants) sessions. Workshop activities will engage these communities in building awareness of the reciprocal benefits of strong partnerships and identifying shared interests, community needs, and barriers to functional partnerships. Participants will develop recommendations for new structures and processes to accelerate the translation of research to innovation and enhance and expand inclusive and experiential training opportunities to prepare a globally competitive, STEM-adaptable workforce.

About the Workshop

The organizing committee has planned a series of virtual and in-person events facilitated with the help of KnowInnovation, a company that specializes in working with science groups to accelerate scientific innovation and achieve actionable outcomes. The agenda is designed to be highly interactive, discussion- and solution-based, with background reading materials provided in advance.

Workshop Goals:

  • Engage academic, private and governmental communities in building awareness of the reciprocal benefits of strong partnerships among stakeholder organizations
  • Identify shared interests, community needs, and barriers to functional partnerships Develop recommendations for new structures and processes that will accelerate the translation of research to innovation
  • Develop recommendations for new structures and processes that will accelerate the translation of research to innovation
  • Enhance and expand inclusive and experiential training opportunities to prepare a globally competitive, STEM-adaptable workforce.

The ultimate anticipated outcome of the workshop are documents that will guide and facilitate use-inspired research and science-informed practices. Documents might include training and career pathway diagrams, recommendations for “use-inspired” tracks for future NSF solicitations, white papers of recommended practices and policies across academic and non-academic organizations, assessments of knowledge of use-inspired research and science-informed practices and opportunities, and logic models that can support use-inspired research across STEM stakeholder communities.

Virtual MicroLabs

A microlab is an interactive, virtual event using the video conferencing platform of Zoom. The goal of the virtual microlab sessions is to identify common needs, benefits, and challenges of academic, private, and government organizations that seek to remain relevant and contribute to advancing science and engineering in the future. During the microlabs, you’ll develop ideas in collaboration with fellow participants.

The in-person Scoping Session will be held at Boise State University June 26 – 28, 2023.
